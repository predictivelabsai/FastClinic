# FastClinic Cockpit

## User Guide — Grow & Activate Your Clinic's Patients

**Modern primary care, made personal.**

Version 0.1.0 · Generated 2026-06-11 · Runs at **fastclinic.example**

A practical guide to using the cockpit to bring patients back, win back lapsed
patients, and grow your clinic — one outreach list at a time.

---

## 1 · What this tool is for

**FastClinic is a patient-activation cockpit for a GP / general-practice clinic.**
It turns the clinic's own visit history into prioritised outreach lists that
bring patients back through the door.

- **The problem it solves:** every clinic quietly loses revenue when
  immunisations lapse, health checks aren't renewed, and good patients drift away
  unnoticed.
- **What it does:** continuously scans your data and surfaces *exactly who to
  contact, why, and when* — with the message already drafted.
- **Who it's for:** practice managers, reception teams, and marketing — no
  technical skill required. If you can read a list and press *Download*, you can
  run a campaign.

> The whole product answers one question: **"Which patients should we contact
> today to grow the clinic — and what do we say?"**

---

## 2 · How it grows your clinic

FastClinic runs a simple, repeatable **activation loop**. Every cycle turns
existing patients into booked appointments and revenue you would otherwise have
lost.

1. **See** — the cockpit ranks who is due, lapsed, or just visited.
2. **Reach** — messages are drafted for you; send by SMS or export.
3. **Book** — patients return for the immunisation, health check, or review.
4. **Measure** — revenue and active-patient counts move; repeat next week.

**Why it compounds:** recurring care (immunisations, annual health checks, repeat
prescriptions) is predictable. Each patient you re-activate this month becomes
due again later — so a steady weekly cadence builds a growing base of returning,
better-cared-for patients.

## 5 · The AI Copilot — just ask

![AI Copilot](img/02-copilot.png)

The right-hand **Copilot** is the fastest way to get an answer or a list. **Just

## 6 · Activation ① — Immunisation & Plan Due

![Immunisation & Plan Due](img/03-reminders.png)

**This is the core engine.** It lists every patient due or overdue for a
recurring service — immunisations, annual health checks, and repeat
prescriptions — most urgent first.

- Filter by service type; see **last date**, **due date**, and **how overdue**.
- Each row carries the **patient's name and phone** — ready to contact.
- A **reminder message is pre-drafted** for the top of the list.
- **Download CSV** to hand to reception or load into SMS.

*Example: many patients due or overdue.* Even a modest booking rate on a list
this size is a direct, repeatable uplift in monthly revenue.

## 7 · Activation ② — Lapsed Reactivation

![Lapsed Reactivation](img/04-lapsed.png)

Win back patients before they're gone for good. This engine finds patients with
**no visit in N months** (adjustable: 6 / 9 / 12 / 18 / 24) and ranks them by
**lifetime value** — so you focus on the patients worth most.

- See **months since last visit**, **total visits**, and **lifetime spend**.
- **"Value at risk"** quantifies the revenue walking out the door.
- Patient name + phone included; a warm win-back message is drafted.

*Example: a list of lapsed patients representing real value at risk.* Reactivating
even a fraction of that is some of the highest-ROI marketing a clinic can do.

---

## 8 · Activation ③ — Post-Visit Follow-up

![Post-Visit Follow-up](img/05-followup.png)

Turn a recent visit into loyalty, a review, and the next booking. This lists
**visits in the last N days** (7 / 14 / 30 / 60) with what was done and any
diagnoses.

- A caring **"how are you feeling?"** message is drafted automatically.
- Great for post-procedure recovery checks, treatment reviews, and gentle rebooking.
- Patient contact included for a personal touch.

**Why it grows the clinic:** follow-up builds trust, lifts reviews and referrals,
and naturally surfaces the next appointment — the cheapest growth there is.

## 9 · Run a campaign in 4 steps

The three activation tabs all work the same way. A full outreach campaign takes
minutes:

1. **Pick a list** — open *Immunisation & Plan Due*, *Lapsed*, or *Follow-up* and
   set the filter (service, months, or window).
2. **Review the draft** — a message is ready at the top; tweak the tone if you like.
3. **Send or export** — **Download CSV** for reception, or push the list to the
   **SMS Broadcaster**.
4. **Measure** — watch *Active patients* and *Revenue* rise on the Overview next week.

Nothing sends automatically — **you stay in control** of every message that goes out.

## 15 · A simple weekly playbook

Consistency beats intensity. A 30-minute weekly rhythm keeps the clinic growing:

- **Monday — Due:** work the *Immunisation & Plan Due* list; send reminders for the most overdue.
- **Wednesday — Lapsed:** pick the top lapsed patients by value; send win-backs.
- **Friday — Follow-up:** check in on the week's visits; invite reviews and rebooks.
- **Monthly:** run the *SEO Audit* and review *Revenue* trends to plan the next promotion.

Small, steady outreach turns the clinic's existing history into a predictable,
growing book of well-cared-for patients.

## 16 · Measuring impact

Track these on the **Overview** week over week — they tell you the loop is working:

- **Active patients (90d)** trending up — more patients coming back.
- **Revenue (90d)** rising — activation converting to bookings.
- **Lapsed count / value at risk** falling — fewer patients slipping away.
- **Visits (30d)** climbing — outreach turning into appointments.

Set a baseline today, run the weekly playbook, and compare in four weeks. The
gains come from revenue you were already leaving on the table.

## Appendix · Data Model

The cockpit reads a single SQLite database built from the clinic's PMS export by
`pms.importer`. The four **raw tables mirror the export 1:1** — every column is
ingested — and two **derived tables** are rolled up for the dashboards. All data
is **synthetic** (generated by `pms.synth`); there is no real patient data.

- **`patient`** — one row per person
- **`diagnosis`** — consultation diagnoses (ICD-10-style)
- **`note`** — consultation notes
- **`item`** — billable line items
- **`consultation`** *(derived)* — one row per visit, rolled up from `item`
- **`client`** *(derived)* — one row per patient's contact record (1:1 with patient)

**Relationships:** `client` 1—1 `patient` 1—∞ `consultation` 1—∞ `item` /
`diagnosis` / `note`, linked by `client_id`, `patient_id`, and `consultation_id`.
A few columns are stored under a business alias the cockpit queries on (noted
below); all others keep their original export name. `evals/run_eval.py` asserts
100% field coverage on every import.

## Appendix · How the data is built

- **Synthetic demo data:** `python -m pms.synth data/synthetic_fastclinic.xlsx 1000`
  — generates a fully synthetic, structurally identical export (no real PHI) that
  the demo auto-builds on first boot.
- **Importer:** `python -m pms.importer <export.xlsx> <out.sqlite>` — idempotent;
  re-run to refresh. Field maps in `pms/importer.py` define the 1:1 ingestion.
- **Catalogue rules:** `pms/catalog.py` classifies each line item into a category
  and flags recurring services (vaccine / health_plan 365d, repeat_prescription
  60d) that drive the activation engines.
- **Coverage guard:** `python -m evals.run_eval` rebuilds the DB and asserts every
  export column is ingested (100% field coverage) alongside the command and route
  regression checks.
